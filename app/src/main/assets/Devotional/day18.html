DAY 18: LET THE WORD OF CHRIST DWELL RICHLY IN YOU

Key Scripture/Memory Verse: Colossians 3:16

“Let the word of Christ dwell in you richly in all wisdom, teaching and admonishing one another in psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, singing with grace in your hearts to the Lord.”

To let the message of Christ dwell richly means to allow His Word to take up residence in our hearts, influencing our thoughts, decisions, and actions. This dwelling is not superficial but involves a deep and transformative absorption of God's wisdom. As we immerse ourselves in the Word, we gain insights that shape our understanding of God's purpose and His ways.

The richness of the Word equips us to teach and admonish one another with wisdom. It becomes a source of guidance and correction in our interactions, fostering a community where believers help each other grow in spiritual maturity. The Word of Christ, dwelling richly within us, becomes a wellspring of wisdom that overflows into our relationships.

Psalms, hymns, and songs from the Spirit become avenues through which we express gratitude to God. Our worship is not merely a collection of words but a heartfelt response to the richness of the Word dwelling within us. The songs we sing become declarations of thanksgiving, echoing the transformative impact of God's message in our lives.

Allowing the Word of Christ to dwell richly within results in an abundantly grateful heart. Gratitude becomes a natural response to the richness of God's love, grace, and wisdom revealed through His Word. As we dwell on the message of Christ, our hearts overflow with thankfulness for the redemptive work accomplished on the cross.

Let God's Word influence your thoughts and actions, and allow the wisdom gleaned from the Scriptures to shape your interactions with others. May the richness of Christ's message be evident in every aspect of your life.

Reflection

Am I allowing the message of Christ to truly dwell richly in me?

Confession

I am grounded and rooted in the reality of this gift and by this gift, I intentionally engage situations and life through the lenses of the mind of Christ.
