DAY 45

NO CONDEMNATION

KEY SCRIPTURE/MEMORY VERSE:

Revelation 12:10
"Then I heard a loud voice saying in heaven, 'Now salvation, and strength, and the kingdom of our God, and the power of His Christ have come, for the accuser of our brethren, who accused them before our God day and night, has been cast down.'

In our text today, Satan is referred to as the accuser of the brethren. Accusations can be more powerful than swords or guns; they attack your identity and make you question who you truly are.

Satan won't confront you directly; instead, he will attempt to accuse you. His goal is to weaken your conviction in Christ and sow doubt about God's love for you. A person who feels accused is already weakened, as accusations obscure your true identity in Christ.

Goliath defied the army of the Living God, asking, "Can you bring a champion out of you?" His words not only demoralized them but prolonged the battle.

Whenever an accusation arises, remember Romans 8:1: there is no condemnation for those who are in Christ. When your identity is threatened, hold onto Romans 8:33: "Who will dare point a finger against us?" You are not just loved; you are accepted in the Beloved (Ephesians 1:6).

REFLECTION
What area of my life have I doubted God's love or acceptance because of accusations?
DECLARATION
Christ has qualified me for every advantage that the believer has; therefore, no weapon of accusation or condemnation fashioned against me can prosper.
