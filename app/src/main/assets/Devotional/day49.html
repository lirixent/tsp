DAY 49 - LOVING GOD

Key Scripture/Memory Verse

1 John 4:19 (NIV)
"We love because he first loved us."

Religion has often taught us to love God as a means to evoke a response from Him. The old covenant emphasized the commandment to love God with all our being. This form of love was duty-bound, rooted in obedience to a command, and often influenced by fear, condemnation, or the desire to avoid punishment.

The new covenant brings a paradigm shift. We love God not out of duty or fear but as a response to His initiative. The foundation of our love is rooted in the fact that He first loved us. It's a relational love, not constrained by fear or condemnation but motivated by the grace and mercy extended to us through Jesus Christ.

Under the new covenant, God has lavished us with all things pertaining to life and godliness. In Christ, there is no condemnation. Our understanding of God's love is shaped by the abundance of His grace, the security found in Christ, and the absence of condemnation. These are the catalysts for our love for Him.

If our love for God is to burn stronger and stronger, we must keep in view how much He has loved us. The realization of His love becomes the fuel for our passion, devotion, and commitment. It transforms our perspective from religious duty to a heartfelt response, making God the consuming fire of our hearts.

As you navigate your day, let the awareness of God's love be the driving force behind your love for Him. Experience a deeper passion as His love consumes your heart, shifting your perspective from duty to a heartfelt response. Recognize that you love Him because He first loved you, and let this truth ignite a flame that burns stronger and stronger.

Reflection

Does the knowledge of God's love influence my response to situations and circumstances?

Confession

I am loved by God!
