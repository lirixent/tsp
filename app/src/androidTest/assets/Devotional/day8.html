DAY 8: PRAYER - THE KNOWLEDGE OF HIS WILL

Key Scripture/Memory Verse: Colossians 1:9 (NIV)

“For this reason, since the day we heard about you, we have not stopped praying for you. We continually ask God to fill you with the knowledge of his will through all the wisdom and understanding that the Spirit gives.”

In Philippians 1:9-10, Paul expresses his earnest prayer for believers—a prayer that their love would abound in knowledge and depth of insight, enabling them to discern what is best. Colossians 1:9 echoes a similar sentiment, emphasizing the continuous prayer for the knowledge of God's will. Let's delve into the transformative power of prayer in illuminating the knowledge of His will.

Both Philippians 1:9-10 and Colossians 1:9 underscore the role of prayer in seeking divine wisdom and understanding. Through prayer, we tap into the Spirit's guidance, gaining insight into the nuances of God's will. It is a recognition that true knowledge of His will is not solely intellectual but a spiritual revelation granted through communion with Him.

The connection between love and knowledge in prayer is significant. Love is not merely an emotion but an active, intentional commitment. Prayer aligns our hearts with God's love, and as our love abounds, so does our knowledge of His will. Love becomes the driving force behind our desire to discern and walk in His perfect plan.

Philippians 1:9 speaks of the desire for discernment—“so that you may be able to discern what is best.” Discernment is a spiritual skill cultivated through prayer, allowing us to navigate the complexities of life and make choices that align with God's will. James 1:5 (NIV) encourages us, “If any of you lacks wisdom, you should ask God, who gives generously to all without finding fault, and it will be given to you.”

Colossians 1:9 emphasizes the continuity of prayer for the knowledge of God's will. Prayer is not a one-time event but a constant dialogue with God, seeking His guidance and aligning our lives with His perfect plan.

Reflection

Do I need discernment in knowing God's will?

Confession

My eyes are open to the knowledge of God's will daily.
