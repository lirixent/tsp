DAY 14: PRAYER - FOR MINISTRY GROWTH AND SUCCESS

Key Scripture/Memory Verse: Romans 1:9-11 (NIV)

“God, whom I serve in my spirit in preaching the gospel of his Son, is my witness how constantly I remember you in my prayers at all times; and I pray that now at last by God's will the way may be opened for me to come to you. I long to see you so that I may impart to you some spiritual gift to make you strong—that is, that you and I may be mutually encouraged by each other's faith.”

The foundation of Paul's prayer is his sincere service to God in spirit, particularly in the proclamation of the gospel. In prayer, we recognize that our service must flow from a genuine connection with God in our innermost being. John 4:24 (NIV) reminds us, “God is spirit, and his worshipers must worship in the Spirit and truth.”

Paul's consistency in remembering others in prayer is a powerful example. In our prayer life, we have the privilege of interceding for fellow believers and ministry endeavors. Colossians 1:9 (NIV) encourages us to pray for others: “For this reason, since the day we heard about you, we have not stopped praying for you.”

Paul's specific prayer is for an open door to come and minister to the believers in Rome. Through prayer, we seek God's guidance and divine opportunities for effective ministry. Colossians 4:3 (NIV) echoes this sentiment: “And pray for us, too, that God may open a door for our message, so that we may proclaim the mystery of Christ.”

Paul's longing to impart spiritual gifts for mutual encouragement underscores the relational aspect of ministry. In prayer, we express our desire for fruitful and edifying relationships within the body of Christ. 1 Thessalonians 5:11 (NIV) encourages mutual encouragement: “Therefore encourage one another and build each other up, just as you are doing.”

The goal of ministry, as expressed in Paul's prayer, is to make others strong in their faith. Prayer becomes a tool for seeking God's empowerment to be an effective instrument in building up and strengthening the body of Christ. Ephesians 4:11-12 (NIV) highlights the purpose of ministry: “So Christ himself gave the apostles, the prophets, the evangelists, the pastors and teachers, to equip his people for works of service, so that the body of Christ may be built up.”

Reflection

Do I contribute to the growth and encouragement of fellow believers?

Confession

I am a source of growth and encouragement to God's people.
