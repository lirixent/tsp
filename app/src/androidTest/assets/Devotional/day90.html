DAY 90
SOURCE OF STRENGTH

KEY SCRIPTURE/MEMORY VERSE: I Thessalonians 5:11 (NIV)

"Therefore encourage one another and build each other up, just as in fact, you are doing."

The New Testament is replete with exhortations to encourage one another. Whether facing trials, challenges, or celebrating victories, believers are called to be a source of strength to those around them. Hebrews 10:24-25 (NIV) emphasizes the importance of considering how to spur one another on toward love and good deeds, not neglecting to meet together for mutual encouragement.

Our words hold the power to uplift, inspire, and provide strength to those who may be going through difficult seasons. Proverbs 16:24 (NIV) beautifully expresses this truth: "Gracious words are a honeycomb, sweet to the soul and healing to the bones." Being intentional about speaking words of encouragement can have a profound impact on the emotional and spiritual well-being of fellow believers.

Being a source of strength extends beyond words to actions. Modeling a life of faith, resilience, and compassion serves as a powerful form of encouragement. Philippians 2:3-4 (NIV) encourages believers to consider others above themselves, looking not only to their interests but also to the interests of others. By living out a Christlike example, we become a source of strength through our deeds.

Interceding for fellow believers is another essential way to be a source of strength. James 5:16 (NIV) underscores the significance of prayer within the community of faith: "Therefore confess your sins to each other and pray for each other so that you may be healed. The prayer of a righteous person is powerful and effective." Prayer binds believers together, providing strength in times of need.

When thoughts of your brother or sister come to your mind, say a prayer for them, reach out to them. In this way, you would be a source of strength to them and edify Christ's body.

REFLECTION

What are some other ways I can serve as a source of strength to other believers?

CONFESSION

My eyes are open to fellow believers and I serve as a source of strength.
