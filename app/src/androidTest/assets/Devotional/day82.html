DAY 82
MASTERING YOUR EMOTIONS

KEY SCRIPTURE/MEMORY VERSE: Colossians 3:15 (NIV)

"Let the peace of Christ rule in your hearts, since as members of one body you were called to peace. And be thankful."

Scripture records the story of the storm, where amidst the chaos and the disciples' attempts to find a way out, Jesus was sleeping. When they woke Him up, filled with the emotions of the dire circumstances, they questioned if He cared about their impending peril. So, what did Jesus do? He arose from His sleep, rebuked the wind, and declared peace (Mark 4:37-40).

Life often mirrors the stormy seas, filled with challenges that evoke a range of emotions. In the story of the storm, the disciples were overwhelmed by fear and anxiety, emotions that threatened to capsize their faith. Similarly, the storms of life can stir emotions within us—fear, doubt, worry, and uncertainty. It is in the midst of these emotional tempests that Colossians 3:15 becomes a guiding light, urging us to let the peace of Christ rule in our hearts.

As the disciples grappled with fear, Jesus, the Master of emotions, arose from His sleep. In the face of chaos, He demonstrated a profound calmness that transcended the circumstances. His response teaches us the importance of mastering our emotions, not allowing them to dictate our faith or cloud our perception of God's love. Jesus' rebuke of the wind and declaration of peace showcase the authority we have in Him to master the storms within.

Colossians 3:15 encourages us to let the peace of Christ rule in our hearts. This goes beyond a fleeting sense of calm; it speaks to a governing force that directs our emotions and responses. When the storms of life threaten to overwhelm us, the peace of Christ becomes the anchor that steadies our hearts. It is a peace that surpasses understanding, a peace that emanates from our relationship with the Prince of Peace.
